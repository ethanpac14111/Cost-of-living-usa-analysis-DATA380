Cost of Living in the USA – Data Analysis Project
Overview
This project analyzes the cost of living across U.S. counties using the US Family Budget Dataset. The goal is to explore patterns in expenses such as housing, transportation, healthcare, childcare, and taxes, and understand how they vary by state and family type.

Dataset
Source: Economic Policy Institute (EPI)
Observations: Approximately 31,430
Variables: 15

Includes data on:

Housing, food, transportation, healthcare

Childcare, taxes, total cost

Median family income

Family size and geographic location

Key Findings
Cost of living varies significantly across states and counties

Transportation and housing are major contributors to total cost

Strong relationship observed between taxes and total cost

Outliers identified in food and childcare costs

PCA and NMF reveal underlying structure in cost components
